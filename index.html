Step 1: Choose Your Concepts

Recommended Theories:

  • Leech (1983): politeness maxims (tact, generosity, approbation, modesty, agreement, sympathy)

Step 2: Draft Your AI Prompts

Goal: Draft AI prompts that help you explore how well the AI explains or demonstrates politeness concepts.

Prompts are questions or tasks you give the AI. These should be carefully designed to test its ability to explain theory clearly and apply it appropriately in realistic contexts.

Prompt Types to Consider:

  • Definition prompts: Ask the AI to define or explain concepts simply or with technical detail
  • Example generation: Ask for sample sentences or dialogues demonstrating a specific strategy
  • Scenario testing: Ask the AI to simulate a polite exchange in a specific situation
  • Comparative tasks: Ask the AI to compare polite vs. impolite versions of the same sentence

Step 3: Design Your Rubric

Goal: Design a robust and thoughtful rubric to evaluate the quality of the AI's output.

A rubric helps you assess AI responses fairly and consistently. You might use a scoring system or written comments for each dimension.

Common Rubric Categories:

  • Accuracy: Are key concepts explained or used correctly according to the theory?
  • Clarity: Is the response understandable to the target audience (e.g., beginner, expert)?
  • Contextual Fit: Are responses appropriate for the situation and the relationship between speakers?
  • Depth and Insight: Does the AI demonstrate deeper understanding, or just surface-level answers?
  • Pragmatic Nuance: Does it reflect social dynamics, like power or distance?
  • Terminology Use: Are theoretical terms defined and used appropriately?
